Resume Assistance

Why is a Strong Resume Important?

A resume is your first impression to recruiters and hiring managers. A well-structured resume:

  • Highlights your skills & achievements professionally.
  • Passes ATS (Applicant Tracking System) filters.
  • Increases your chances of getting shortlisted.
  • Differentiates you from other candidates.

Common Resume Mistakes & How to Fix Them

  • Using a generic resume: Customize for each job with keywords.
  • Including unnecessary details: Only add name, phone, email, LinkedIn.
  • Poor formatting & long paragraphs: Use bullet points and proper spacing.
  • Not including numbers & results: Show impact with measurable results.
  • Spelling & grammatical mistakes: Proofread and use Grammarly.

What Should Be Included in Your Resume?

  • Contact Information: Name, Phone, Email, LinkedIn, GitHub.
  • Resume Summary: A short professional introduction.
  • Skills: Technical and soft skills relevant to the job.
  • Work Experience: Job title, company, duration, key achievements.
  • Education: Degree, university, graduation year.
  • Projects: Technologies used, accomplishments.
  • Certifications: Relevant courses and achievements.
